Project Lead

Heterogeneous Resource-Aware Distributed Agent System

Jun 2025 - Sep 2025
Core Member

As a core contributor, I helped decompose agent workflows into fine-grained tasks and compile them into heterogeneous DAGs across GPU, CPU, and I/O resources. I integrated a HACS-style scheduler that places tasks based on compute, memory, and communication characteristics rather than coarse agent-level rules. On a 32-node A100 cluster, the system achieved up to 6.2x throughput improvement and a 71.2% reduction in P95 tail latency, with GPU memory utilization improved by 2x+. For dynamic workflows, P95 latency was reduced by 22.3% vs. Ray and 47.8% vs. AutoGen. I also led external API standardization and benchmark evaluation on GAIA, tau-bench, and OpenAGI, supporting a co-authored OSDI 2026 submission and open-source impact of 400+ GitHub stars.

GitHub: maze-agent/Maze

Weakly Supervised Fine-Grained Recognition (National First Prize)

Oct 2025 - Nov 2025
Project Lead

I designed and led a two-stage, cross-architecture knowledge distillation pipeline, where a fine-tuned ConvNeXtV2 teacher supervised a DINOv2 student under a joint KL-divergence and GCE objective. With AdamW optimization, cosine annealing, and DDP multi-GPU training, the model achieved 97.3% accuracy on WebFG-400 and 90.6% on WebiNat-5000. This work earned a National First Prize and demonstrated that a carefully engineered training strategy can deliver both strong generalization and state-of-the-art competitive performance.
